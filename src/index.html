Famous Islands


  • Hawaii, USA

    An iconic volcanic island chain known for its beaches, surfing, and rich Polynesian culture.

    It's located in the central Pacific Ocean.

  • Greenland

    The world’s largest island (not considered a continent), located between the Arctic and Atlantic Oceans.

    It is known for its vast ice sheets.

  • Madagascar, Africa

    Located off the southeast coast of Africa, it is the fourth-largest island in the world

    and home to unique wildlife found nowhere else.

  • Galápagos Islands, Ecuador

    Known for their unique animal species and role in Charles Darwin’s theory of evolution.

    They are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • New Zealand (North & South Islands)

    Composed mainly of two large islands, New Zealand is famous for its diverse landscapes, Maori culture,

    and filming locations for movies like Lord of the Rings.

Types of Islands Examples


  1. Volcanic: Hawaii (formed over a hotspot in the Pacific Plate), Iceland (formed at the Mid-Atlantic Ridge)
  2. Continental: Greenland, New Guinea, Madagascar
  3. Barrier: Outer Banks in North Carolina, USA
  4. Coral: The Maldives, Bikini Atoll
  5. Artificial: Palm Jumeirah in Dubai
